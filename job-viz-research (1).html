Step 2 — Reference
Visualization Pattern Library
20 chart patterns researched for job market data. Each includes use case, strengths, and caveats for your specific platform.
Line Chart + Threshold TREND
Plots job posting volume over time with a configurable threshold line that highlights sudden spikes or drops in demand.
Best for: Detecting hiring surges, identifying role decline. Works with continuous time-series data.
Time SeriesQuantitative
Sankey Diagram SHIFT
Shows how roles or skills flow and transform over time. Ideal for showing how one job title category splits into multiple emerging roles.
Best for: Role evolution, title fragmentation, skill overlap between positions.

Ranked List + Movement Indicators
Shows job roles ranked by volume, with delta indicators (▲▼ NEW) showing change from prior period. Scannable and action-oriented.
Best for: Weekly/monthly role demand snapshots. Users instantly see what's rising, falling, or brand new.
OrdinalDiscrete
Heatmap Matrix
Shows which skills appear most frequently across different job title categories. Color intensity = demand. Great for comparing skill overlap.
Best for: Skill-to-role mapping, identifying skill gaps, showing overlap between "similar" roles.
QuantitativeStructured
Bump Chart / Ranking Over Time
Tracks how role rankings shift across time periods. Lines crossing each other visually communicate role overtaking — dramatic and readable.
Best for: Quarterly role demand rankings, which skills are gaining vs losing priority over time.
OrdinalTime Series
Treemap
Shows proportional distribution of job categories by area. Instantly communicates market composition and relative size of each role cluster.
Best for: Job market breakdown by category, platform composition, skill demand share.
QuantitativeNominal
Stream Graph
An organic, flowing area chart showing how multiple role categories evolve in volume simultaneously. Good for showing market composition shift.
Best for: Category-level job market shifts over time. Shows rise of AI roles visually overtaking legacy roles.
Time SeriesContinuous
Network / Force Graph
Shows how job roles connect through shared skills. Roles that share many skills cluster together. Reveals hidden similarities between different-named roles.
Best for: Role similarity detection — showing "ML Engineer" and "AI Engineer" are functionally the same.
QualitativeRelationship
Bubble / Scatter Plot
Maps roles by two axes (e.g. volume vs. growth rate), with bubble size as a third variable (e.g. median salary). Great for quadrant analysis.
Best for: "High growth, low volume" emerging roles vs "high volume, stable" mature roles quadrant view.
ContinuousQuantitative
